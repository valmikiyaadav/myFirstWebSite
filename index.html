Rāmāyana is one of the two major Sanskrit epics of ancient India, the other being the
Mahābhārata. Along with the Mahābhārata, it forms the Hindu Itihasa.
The epic, traditionally ascribed to the Maharishi Valmiki, narrates the life of Rama,
a legendary prince of Ayodhya city in the kingdom of Kosala.
